Structure of an Airplane

Main Components:-

  1. Cockpit: Main section where pilot sits.
  2. Fuselage: Section where travellers sit.
  3. Slats: Attached to the front of wings which adds to the lift.
  4. Spoiler: Plates on the surface of wings which helps in reducing the lift.
  5. Aileron: Hinged at the back of wings, helping in rolling movement. (They move vice-versa)
  6. Flaps: Used to alter the characterstics of lift.
  7. Elevator: Attached at tail section helps in pitch up and pitch down.
  8. Rudder: Helps in turning left and right without rolling (yawing movement).
  9. Wing: Gives lift to an aircraft.
  10. Jet engine: Creates thrust and keep the plane moving in air.

How a plane rolls, pitch and yaw:

Movement Component Used Visual
Rolling (Roll left-right) Ailerons
Pitching (Nose up-down) Elevator
Yawing (Turn left-right) Rudder
  • Left aileron UP/DOWN = Plane Rolls LEFT/RIGHT
  • Elevator UP/DOWN = Nose UP/DOWN
  • Rudder LEFT/RIGHT = Plane Turns LEFT/RIGHT
